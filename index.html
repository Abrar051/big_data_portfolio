Why February–April?

Winter ventilation is reduced to retain heat. Litter moisture accumulates over months. When spring arrives, bacterial decomposition accelerates rapidly — driving NH3 spikes.

Recommendation
  • → Increase airflow from 1 February each year
  • → Daily NH3 checks during Feb, Mar, Apr
  • → Pre-season litter replacement in January
  • → Automated NH3 trigger at 25 ppm year-round

Key Finding – Correlation Analysis

Pearson correlations reveal co-production links and environmental drivers across all nodes

NH3 ↔ NO2
r = 0.660 — Strong
Both gases co-produced by organic litter decomposition. When NH3 rises, NO2 rises simultaneously. A single ventilation trigger at 25 ppm NH3 will protect workers against NO2 exposure too.
⚠️ Most actionable correlation in the dataset
Humidity → NH3
r = 0.243 — Moderate
Moist air creates damp bedding, accelerating bacterial decomposition and ammonia release. Humidity control directly suppresses NH3.
→ Target humidity 50–70% RH to reduce NH3 risk
Temperature → TVOC
r = 0.320 — Moderate
Higher temperatures drive volatile organic compound evaporation from litter and waste. Peak TVOC exposure aligns with midday heat.
→ Increase ventilation during 10:00–15:00 daily
PM2.5 ↔ PM10
r = 0.997 — Near-perfect
Both particle sizes share an identical source — farm dust, feathers and litter disturbance. Treat as a single dust risk. One intervention (litter moisture control) addresses both.
→ PM2.5 and PM10 can be managed together

Recommendations

CRITICAL
Backup Heating System

Node 2 heating failure during winter caused 14,133 outage readings. Install with automated alerts to prevent recurrence during the Nov–Jan critical window.

CRITICAL
Controlled Ventilation System

Trigger at 25 ppm NH3. Increase ventilation rates during 10:00–15:00 peak period to mitigate animal welfare risks and simultaneously address NO2 exposure.

CRITICAL
Baby Chick Temperature Adjustment

Raise thermostat by 1–2°C to prevent cold stress. AAKTP1 current avg 28.74°C vs 29°C minimum recommended by Aviagen (2018).

HIGH
Litter Management Protocol

Address PM2.5 and PM10 simultaneously through improved litter moisture and dust control. Given r = 0.997 correlation, one intervention resolves both parameters.

HIGH
Real-Time Monitoring Dashboard

Deploy threshold classification system with integrated CO2 and CO sensor data for comprehensive environmental monitoring. Current regulatory gap must be addressed.
